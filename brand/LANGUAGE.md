# Signal Loop identity language

## Position

Recursive Harness is durable infrastructure for agents that learn through evidence. The
brand should feel observant, exact, calm under pressure, and unmistakably technical.

**Name:** Recursive Harness

**Endorsement:** A Ghostly agent system

**Promise:** Durable state. Observable agents. Evidence-backed improvement.

## Visual grammar

- **Recursive signal:** one continuous loop, never a decorative infinity glyph.
- **Four nodes:** predict, act, score, learn. Their sequence is stable.
- **Ledger trail:** small repeated records extending from the loop; evidence accumulates.
- **Guard boundary:** a thin enclosing perimeter; control is visible without feeling carceral.
- **Ghost signal:** intelligence implied through light, negative space, or interference—not a
  cartoon character.
- **HUD restraint:** registration marks and scanlines establish provenance and observation;
  they never compete with content.

## Color and type

Use `tokens.json` as the source of truth. Near-black is the canvas, cobalt establishes
depth, cyan carries active signals, ice white carries primary text, and violet is a rare
secondary accent. Warm beige, brown, orange, and red do not belong in the core identity.

Use a modern sans-serif stack for prose and a monospace stack for IDs, commands, labels,
and measurements. Uppercase tracking is reserved for short system labels.

## Motion and composition

Motion follows the loop in one direction and leaves an evidence trail. Avoid random glitch,
constant flicker, parallax for its own sake, or dense “cyber” decoration. Hero compositions
may be cinematic; operational diagrams remain flat, exact, and readable.

## Voice

Lead with outcomes and verifiable behavior. Prefer “records a scored prediction” over
“supercharges intelligence.” Never imply that model weights change, that an illustration is
a product screenshot, or that an unreviewed observation is learned policy.
